Mexican Food

Mexican cuisine's ingredients and methods arise from the area's first agricultural communities, such as those of the Olmec and Maya, who domesticated maize, created the standard process of nixtamalization, and established foodways. Successive waves of other Mesoamerican groups brought with them their cooking methods.

Popular Foods

Tacos

Tacos are a traditional Mexican dish featuring a small, hand-sized corn or wheat tortilla folded around a filling, eaten by hand as a popular street food. Fillings vary widely but often include seasoned meats (beef, pork, chicken, seafood), beans, or vegetables, topped with condiments like salsa, onion, cilantro, and sometimes cheese or guacamole, offering a versatile blend of flavors and textures.

Mole

Mole is a traditional, complex Mexican sauce or concoction known for its deep, layered flavors, made from a blend of ingredients like chili peppers, fruits, nuts, seeds, spices, and sometimes chocolate, resulting in a rich, thick sauce often served over meat for special occasions

Enchiladas

Enchiladas are a classic Mexican dish of corn tortillas rolled around a filling (like meat, cheese, beans, or vegetables) and covered in a savory chili-based sauce, often baked until hot and bubbly.
